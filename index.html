Detailed Analysis

All 18 Findings

Each finding is categorized by severity and includes a specific, actionable recommendation for improvement.

🔴 Critical Issues
Must fix immediately
1
No clear primary call-to-action above the fold
The donate button is buried in the navigation alongside 5 other links, with no visual distinction. First-time visitors can't immediately understand what action to take.
💡 Create a single, prominent "Donate Today" button in a high-contrast color (e.g. warm red or amber) placed in the top-right nav and repeated in the hero section. Remove nav clutter.
Critical
2
Hero section fails to communicate mission impact
The hero headline leads with the organization's full name rather than the mission or impact. Users don't understand "why this matters to me" within 5 seconds of landing.
💡 Rewrite headline to lead with outcome: "Transforming 12,000 lives through education, health, and economic opportunity." Follow with a 1-sentence description, then CTA.
Critical
3
Website is not mobile-responsive
The layout does not adapt to smaller screens. Navigation wraps awkwardly, text becomes unreadable, and tap targets are under 30px — failing WCAG 2.1 guidelines. 60%+ of nonprofit web traffic is mobile.
💡 Implement responsive breakpoints using CSS Grid or Flexbox. Minimum tap target size: 44×44px. Collapse navigation into a hamburger menu on mobile. Test on actual devices.
Critical
4
Text contrast ratio fails accessibility standards
Small grey text on light backgrounds achieves only a 2.8:1 contrast ratio. WCAG AA requires 4.5:1 for normal text. This excludes users with visual impairments and affects readability for all.
💡 Audit all color pairs using a tool like WebAIM Contrast Checker. Darken body text to #333333 minimum. Ensure all form labels and links meet 4.5:1. Large headings need minimum 3:1.
Critical
🟡 Major Issues
Address in next sprint
5
No social proof or impact data on homepage
The homepage lacks numbers, testimonials, or stories. Donors need evidence of real-world impact. Without it, trust and conversion rates suffer significantly.
💡 Add an impact bar with 3 key metrics (families served, programs funded, years active). Include one short quote from a beneficiary with photo. These dramatically increase donor confidence.
Major
6
Navigation information architecture is confusing
"About Us," "Programs," and "News" are generic labels that don't guide users toward meaningful actions. The IA reflects internal org structure rather than user goals.
💡 Restructure nav around user goals: "Our Work" (programs), "Our Impact" (stories + data), "Get Involved" (donate/volunteer), "About" (org info). Run a card sorting exercise with actual users.
Major
7
Weak and generic calls-to-action throughout
"Click Here to Learn More," "Submit," and "Read More" provide no context or urgency. They fail to communicate value or differentiate one action from another.
💡 Use specific, benefit-driven CTAs: "Fund a Child's Education," "See How We Helped Maria," "Join 500 Monthly Donors." Each CTA should connect to a specific outcome.
Major
8
Outdated news section with stale content
News items are over a year old. Stale content signals organizational inactivity and erodes trust. Users assume the org may no longer be operational or engaged.
💡 Either maintain fresh content (at least bi-monthly posts) or replace the news section with an "Impact Stories" section that doesn't date poorly. Add a newsletter subscription CTA.
Major
9
No donation progress indicator or recurring giving option
The donation page (if it exists) lacks context about what donations achieve. No monthly giving option is prominently offered, missing a key recurring revenue opportunity.
💡 Add a donation amount selector with impact equivalences ("$25 = school supplies for one child"). Offer monthly giving as the default option. Show a campaign progress bar.
Major
🔵 Moderate Issues
Plan for upcoming quarter
10
Typography lacks hierarchy and is hard to scan
The site uses only 1-2 font sizes and weights, making it difficult to skim. Important information is treated the same as secondary content. Line length exceeds 80 characters on desktop.
💡 Establish a clear type scale: Display (hero, 48–64px), H1 (32px), H2 (24px), Body (16-17px, max-width 680px). Vary weight (400 for body, 600 for headings). Line height 1.6–1.7 for readability.
Moderate
11
Missing Open Graph and social sharing metadata
When links are shared on social media, no image, title, or description is populated. This results in blank previews that dramatically reduce click-through rates from social posts.
💡 Add og:title, og:description, og:image, and og:url meta tags to every page. Use a 1200×630px image. This is a 30-minute fix with significant reach impact.
Moderate
12
No volunteer or partnership pathway
Users who want to support without donating have no clear journey. Volunteers and corporate partners are a critical nonprofit resource that the site entirely ignores.
💡 Add a "Get Involved" hub with three paths: Donate, Volunteer, Corporate Partner. Each path should have its own landing page with a clear sign-up or inquiry form.
Moderate
13
Footer is cluttered and unhelpful
The footer contains 10+ navigation links in an unorganized paragraph format, plus 6 social media links. Critical trust signals (charity registration, annual report) are absent.
💡 Redesign footer with 3 columns: About/Quick Links, Contact/Social, Trust Signals (charity number, Guidestar rating, SSL badge). Include a newsletter sign-up. Max 5 links per column.
Moderate
14
No search functionality for content-heavy pages
As the site grows, users cannot find specific programs, reports, or news. This creates friction and increases bounce rates from users who don't find what they need.
💡 Add a search bar to the navigation (collapsible on mobile). Consider implementing Google Custom Search or a lightweight JS search like Lunr.js for static sites.
Moderate
15
Images lack alt text and are decorative without purpose
Hero and body images have no descriptive alt text. Images don't appear to be real photos of actual work — stock imagery reduces authenticity and emotional connection.
💡 Replace stock photos with real community/program photos. Write descriptive alt text for all meaningful images. Decorative images get empty alt="". This is both an accessibility and trust issue.
Moderate
16
Slow page load speed hurts both UX and SEO
Unoptimized images and no lazy loading cause slow initial page loads. Google's Core Web Vitals penalize slow sites in search ranking. Users abandon pages that take over 3 seconds to load.
💡 Compress images with WebP format. Implement lazy loading for below-fold images. Minify CSS/JS. Use a CDN. Target LCP under 2.5 seconds. Tools: Google PageSpeed, GTmetrix.
Moderate
17
No analytics or conversion tracking in place
Without analytics, improvement is guesswork. There's no data on which pages convert, where users drop off, or which traffic sources drive donations.
💡 Install Google Analytics 4 with goal tracking for donations, newsletter signups, and contact form submissions. Add Microsoft Clarity for heatmaps and session recordings to identify friction.
Moderate
18
No multilingual support despite diverse communities served
If the foundation serves diverse communities, offering only English excludes potential beneficiaries and donors. This is a strategic gap, not just a UX issue.
💡 At minimum, add a language toggle for the top 2 languages of your served community. Google Translate integration is a quick start. Full translation of key pages (home, donate, programs) is the goal.

Design Comparison

Before vs. After: Key UX Transformations

Side-by-side breakdown of the most impactful design changes and the reasoning behind each decision.

❌ Current State — Navigation
  • 6 equally-weighted nav items with no visual hierarchy
  • "Donate Now" treated like any other page link
  • Long organization name crowds the header
  • No search or language toggle
  • Collapses poorly on mobile — unusable
✅ Proposed Design — Navigation
  • 4 goal-oriented nav items: Our Work, Impact, Get Involved, About
  • "Donate" is a high-contrast button — visually dominant
  • Short logo with distinctive icon builds brand recall
  • Collapses to clean hamburger menu on mobile
  • Sticky on scroll so CTA is always accessible
❌ Current State — Hero Section
  • Headline leads with organization's long name
  • Generic stock image or flat gradient background
  • Dense paragraph text with no scannable structure
  • CTA "Click Here to Learn More" — vague and passive
  • No impact numbers or proof of work
✅ Proposed Design — Hero Section
  • Headline leads with mission impact and human benefit
  • Real community photo adds authenticity and emotion
  • 1-sentence description, then CTA — no clutter
  • CTA: "See Our Impact →" or "Fund a Family Today"
  • Impact stats bar immediately below: families served, $raised, years
❌ Current State — Content Layout
  • Three equal columns: Programs, News, Contact — no hierarchy
  • Contact info in the body (belongs in footer)
  • News items are 1+ year old — signals inactivity
  • No visual cards or structure — walls of text
  • Programs described generically with no specifics
✅ Proposed Design — Content Layout
  • Program cards with icons, name, and 1-line descriptor
  • Each card has a "Learn More" link to dedicated program page
  • Impact stories section with real beneficiary quotes + photos
  • Newsletter CTA replaces stale news widget
  • Clear visual weight: Programs > Stories > Partners

Phase 1 — Quick Wins & Critical Fixes

🎯Rewrite hero headline and hero CTA button (2 hours)
🎨Fix all contrast ratios — audit with WebAIM tool (3 hours)
📊Install Google Analytics 4 with donation goal tracking (2 hours)
🔗Add Open Graph metadata to all pages (1 hour)
✍️Update all CTAs with specific, action-oriented language (2 hours)
📰Update or remove stale news content (1 hour)
Week 3–6

Phase 2 — Core UX Redesign

📱Implement responsive CSS — mobile-first redesign of all key pages
🗺️Restructure navigation IA based on user goals (run card sort)
💯Design and add impact stats bar to homepage
🖼️Replace stock images with real community photography
♿Add ARIA labels, keyboard navigation, screen reader testing
👣Redesign footer with organized columns and trust signals

Phase 3 — Strategic Growth Features

📈A/B test donation CTAs, hero headlines, and button colors
